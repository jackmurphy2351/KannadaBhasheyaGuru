# A Grammar Guide to Kannada Verb Conjugation (ಕಾಲಗಳು ಮತ್ತು ಕ್ರಿಯಾಪದಗಳು)

## Overview

In Kannada, mastering the verb is 80% of the battle. Once you know the root and the ending, you can construct almost any sentence.

**The Golden Formula:**
`Verb Root` + `Tense Marker` + `Pronominal Ending` = **Conjugated Verb**

---

## 1. The Pronominal Endings (The "Tail")

These are the suffixes that tell you *who* is doing the action. They attach to the end of the verb. Because these endings are unique to the person, you can technically drop the pronoun (Subject) and still be understood.

| Person | Pronoun | Meaning | Ending (Formal/Written) | Ending (Colloquial/Spoken) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Sing** | **Nānu** (ನಾನು) | I | **-ēne** (-ಏನೆ) | **-īni** (-ಈನಿ) |
| **2nd Sing** | **Nīnu** (ನೀನು) | You (informal) | **-iya** (-ಇಯ) | **-ya** / **-i** (-ಯ/-ಇ) |
| **2nd Hon** | **Nīvu** (ನೀವು) | You (polite/pl) | **-iri** (-ಇರಿ) | **-tīri** (-ತೀರಿ) |
| **3rd Masc** | **Avanu** (ಅವನು) | He | **-āne** (-ಆನೆ) | **-āne** (-ಆನೆ) |
| **3rd Fem** | **Avaḷu** (ಅವಳು) | She | **-āḷe** (-ಆಳೆ) | **-āḷe** (-ಆಳೆ) |
| **3rd Neut** | **Adu** (ಅದು) | It | **-ade** (-ಅದೆ) | **-utte** (-ಉತ್ತೆ) |
| **1st Pl** | **Nāvu** (ನಾವು) | We | **-ēve** (-ಏವೆ) | **-tīvi** (-ತೀವಿ) |
| **3rd Pl** | **Avaru** (ಅವರು) | They (human) | **-āre** (-ಆರೆ) | **-tāre** (-ತಾರೆ) |

---


## 2. The Present Tense (Non-Past)

**Usage:** Used for habits, general truths, and future actions.
*Note: In spoken Kannada, we rarely use a separate "Future Tense." If you say "I go to the shop" (Hōgtīni), it implies you will go.*

* **The Marker:** **-utta** (-ಉತ್ತ)
* **Structure:** `Root` + `utta` + `Ending`

**Example: Māḍu (ಮಾಡು - To do)**

| Pronoun | Written/Formal | Spoken/Conversational | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nānu** | **Māḍuttēne** | **Māḍtīni** | I do / I will do |
| **Nīnu** | **Māḍuttīya** | **Māḍtiya** | You do |
| **Nīvu** | **Māḍuttīri** | **Māḍtīri** | You (resp) do |
| **Avanu** | **Māḍuttāne** | **Māḍtāne** | He does |
| **Avaḷu** | **Māḍuttāḷe** | **Māḍtāḷe** | She does |
| **Adu** | **Māḍuttade** | **Māḍutte** | It does |
| **Nāvu** | **Māḍuttēve** | **Māḍtīvi** | We do |
| **Avaru** | **Māḍuttāre** | **Māḍtāre** | They do |

**Quick Tip for Hindi Speakers:**
* **Habitual (*-ta*):** Just as Hindi uses **-tā** for habits (*Main kar**tā** hoon*), Kannada uses **-utta** (*Nānu māḍ**utta** ēne* $\rightarrow$ *Māḍuttēne* $\rightarrow$ *Māḍtīni*).
* **Continuous (*-rahā*):** Be careful! If you elongate the sound to **-uttā** and add *iddīni*, it becomes Continuous (*Main kar **rahā** hoon*). See Section 4 below for an explication of the Continuous.
    * *Māḍtīni* = *Kartā hoon* (I do)
    * *Māḍtā iddīni* = *Kar rahā hoon* (I am doing)

## 3. The Past Tense

**Usage:** Completed actions.
* **The Marker:** Usually **-da** (-ದ), but irregulars exist.

### Group A: Regular Verbs (The 'u' verbs)
Most verbs ending in 'u' take a **-d-** sound.
* **Māḍu** (māḍu) $\rightarrow$ **Māḍida** (māḍida) + ending

### Group B: Irregular / Strong Verbs
These must be memorized, but they are the most common verbs.

| Verb Root | Meaning | Past Stem | Example (I did...) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** | To do | **Māḍida** | **Nānu māḍide** (I did) |
| **Baru** | To come | **Banda** | **Nānu bande** (I came) |
| **Hōgu** | To go | **Hōda** | **Nānu hōde** (I went) |
| **Tinnu** | To eat | **Tinda** | **Nānu tinde** (I ate) |
| **Iru** | To be | **Idda** | **Nānu idde** (I was) |
| **Kāṇu** | To see | **Kaṇḍa** | **Nānu kaṇḍe** (I saw) |
| **Koḍu** | To give | **Koṭṭa** | **Nānu koṭṭe** (I gave) |

**Structure:** `Past Stem` + `Ending (Short form)`
*(Note: In Past tense, the 'I' ending is usually just 'e', not 'ēne')*

**Example: Hōgu (To go) $\rightarrow$ Stem: Hōda**
* **Nānu hōde** - I went
* **Nīvu hōdri** - You went (Plural/Resp)
* **Avanu hōda** - He went
* **Avaḷu hōdaḷu** - She went
* **Adu hōyitu** - It went (*Irregular neuter ending*)

---

## 4. The Present Continuous (Right Now)

**Usage:** Actions happening at this very moment.
* **Structure:** `Root` + `tā` + `iru (conjugated)`

**The Formula:**
1.  Take Verb Root (e.g., **Baru**)
2.  Add **-tā** (**Bartā** - coming)
3.  Add "To Be" (**Iddīni** / **Iddāne** / etc.)

| Person | Kannada (Spoken) | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I am...** | **Nānu bartā iddīni** | I am coming. |
| **You are...** | **Nīvu bartā iddīri** | You are coming. |
| **He is...** | **Avanu bartā iddāne** | He is coming. |
| **She is...** | **Avaḷu bartā iddāḷe** | She is coming. |
| **It is...** | **Maḷe bartā ide** | It is raining (*Rain is coming*). |

---

## 5. Important Verbs: To Be (Iru) vs. To Become (Āgu)

These two confuse everyone.

### A. Iru (ಇರು) - Existence / Location
Used for "I am here," "I have," "It is in the box."
* **Present:** Nānu illē **iddīni**. (I am right here.)
* **Past:** Nānu nenne ūralli **idde**. (I was in town yesterday.)

### B. Āgu (ಆಗು) - To Happen / To Become
Used for events, feelings, or finishing things.
* **Present/Future:** Adu **āgutte**. (It will happen / It happens.)
* **Past:** Adu **āytu**. (It happened / It is finished.)

**Conversational Gem:**
When you finish a task (like eating or working), Kannadigas don't say "I finished." They say "It happened."
* **Ūṭa āytā?** - *Literal: Did meal happen?* (Meaning: Have you eaten?)
* **Hū, āytu.** - *Yes, it happened.* (Meaning: Yes, I ate.)

| Tense | Kannada | Transliteration | English |

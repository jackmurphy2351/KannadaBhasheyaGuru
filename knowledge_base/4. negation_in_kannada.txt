
# Comprehensive Guide to Kannada Negation (ಕನ್ನಡದಲ್ಲಿ ನಿಷೇಧ)

## 1. The Core Distinction: Alla (ಅಲ್ಲ) vs. Illa (ಇಲ್ಲ)

This is the most important rule in Kannada negation. If you mix these up, the meaning changes completely.

### A. ಅಲ್ಲ (Alla) - Negation of Identity / Quality
Use **Alla** to negate nouns or adjectives. It means "is not X" (i.e., it is something else).
* **Rule:** (Subject) + (Noun/Adjective) + **alla**
* **Sandhi Note:** When adding *alla* to a word ending in a vowel, you usually join them with a 'y' or 'v' sound (e.g., *Mane* + *alla* = *Maneyalla*).

| Context | Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Object** | ಇದು ನನ್ನ ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲ. | **Idu nanna maneyalla.** | This is not my house. |
| **Profession** | ನಾನು ಡಾಕ್ಟರ್ ಅಲ್ಲ. | **Nānu ḍākṭar alla.** | I am not a doctor. |
| **Quality** | ಊಟ ಖಾರವಲ್ಲ. | **Ūṭa khāravalla.** | The meal is not spicy. |

### B. ಇಲ್ಲ (Illa) - Negation of Existence / Action
Use **Illa** to negate verbs or existence. It means "is not there," "does not exist," or "didn't happen."
* **Rule:** (Subject) + (Location/Verb) + **illa**

| Context | Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Existence** | ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಯಾರೂ ಇಲ್ಲ. | **Maneyalli yārū illa.** | No one is in the house. |
| **Possession** | ನನಗೆ ಸಮಯವಿಲ್ಲ. | **Nanage samayavilla.** | I don't have time. (*Samaya* + *illa*) |
| **Action** | ಅವನು ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡಲ್ಲ. | **Avanu kelasa māḍalla.** | He does not work. |

## 2. Negating Verbs (Simple Tenses)

The best part of simple negative tenses in Kannada is that **the verb form does not change** for any pronoun (I, you, he, she, we, they).

### A. Simple Present & Future ("Don't / Won't")
In conversation, the present and future negatives are the same. Context (like *nāḷe* - tomorrow) clarifies the tense.

* **Formal Rule:** Verb Root + *-uvudilla*
* **Colloquial Rule:** Verb Root + *-alla*

| Verb | Kannada (Spoken) | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** (To do) | ನಾನು ಮಾಡಲ್ಲ. | **Nānu māḍalla.** | I don't/won't do. |
| **Baru** (To come) | ಅವರು ನಾಳೆ ಬರಲ್ಲ. | **Avaru nāḷe baralla.** | They won't come tomorrow. |
| **Hōgu** (To go) | ನಾನು ಹೋಗಲ್ಲ. | **Nānu hōgalla.** | I won't go. |

### B. Simple Past ("Did not")
This form is also universal for all pronouns.
* **Rule:** Verb Stem + *-lilla*

| Verb | Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kēḷu** (To ask) | ಅವಳು ಕೇಳಲಿಲ್ಲ. | **Avaḷu kēḷalilla.** | She did not ask. |
| **Kaluhi** (To send) | ನಾವು ಕಳುಹಿಸಲಿಲ್ಲ. | **Nāvu kaḷuhisalilla.** | We did not send. |
| **Baru** (To come) | ಬಸ್ ಬರಲಿಲ್ಲ. | **Bas baralilla.** | The bus did not come. |

## 3. Negating Aspects (Continuous & Perfect)

### A. Present Continuous ("Is not doing")
Used for actions not happening right now.
* **Rule:** Verb Root + *-tā* + **illa**

* **Example:** ಈಗ ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ.
    * **Īga maḷe bartā illa.** (It is not raining right now.)
* **Example:** ಬೆಕ್ಕು ಮಲಗ್ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ.
    * **Bekku malagtā illa.** (The cat is not sleeping.)

### B. Past Continuous ("Was not doing")
Used for actions that were not in progress in the past.
* **Rule:** Verb Root + *-tā* + **iralilla**

* **Example:** ನಾನು ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡ್ತಾ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ.
    * **Nānu kelasa māḍtā iralilla.** (I was not working.)

### C. Present Perfect ("Have not done")
**Crucial Distinction:**
* *Māḍalilla* = I didn't do it (at that specific moment).
* *Māḍilla* = I haven't done it (yet/ever).

* **Rule:** Past Participle (ends in -i/-du) + **illa**

| Verb | Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** | ನಾನು ಇನ್ನೂ ಮಾಡಿಲ್ಲ. | **Nānu innū māḍilla.** | I haven't done it yet. |
| **Hōgu** | ಅವಳು ಹೋಗಿಲ್ಲ. | **Avaḷu hōgilla.** | She hasn't gone. |
| **Nōḍu** | ನಾನು ಆ ಸಿನಿಮಾ ನೋಡಿಲ್ಲ. | **Nānu ā sinimā nōḍilla.** | I haven't seen that movie. |

## 4. Negating Moods & Common Constructions

### A. Imperative ("Don't!")
Used for commands.
* **Singular/Informal:** Root + **bēḍa**
* **Plural/Respect:** Root + **bēḍi**

* **Example:** ಅಲ್ಲಿಗೆ ಹೋಗಬೇಡ! (**Allige hōgabēḍa!**) - Don't go there!
* **Example:** ಚಿಂತೆ ಮಾಡಬೇಡಿ. (**Cinte māḍabēḍi.**) - Don't worry.

### B. Prohibitive ("Should not")
Used for general rules or moral prohibitions.
* **Rule:** Root + **bāradu**

* **Example:** ಸುಳ್ಳು ಹೇಳಬಾರದು. (**Suḷḷu hēḷabāradu.**) - One should not tell lies.

### C. Potential / Ability ("Cannot")
Used to express inability.
* **Rule:** Infinitive (*-alu* / *-akke*) + **āgalla**

* **Example:** ನನಗೆ ಅರ್ಥಮಾಡಿಕೊಳ್ಳಲು ಆಗಲ್ಲ.
    * **Nanage arthamāḍikoḷḷalu āgalla.** (I cannot understand.)
* **Example:** ಅವನಿಗೆ ಬರಕ್ಕೆ ಆಗಲ್ಲ.
    * **Avanige barakke āgalla.** (He cannot come.)

### D. Desire ("Don't want")
* **Rule:** Dative Subject + Object + **bēḍa**

* **Example:** ನನಗೆ ಕಾಫಿ ಬೇಡ. (**Nanage kāphi bēḍa.**) - I don't want coffee.
* **Example:** ನನಗೆ ಹೋಗಲು ಬೇಡ. (**Nanage hōgalu bēḍa.**) - I don't want to go.

### E. Obligation ("Don't have to")
* **Rule:** Root + **bēkāgilla**

* **Example:** ನೀವು ಬರಬೇಕಾಗಿಲ್ಲ. (**Nīvu barabēkāgilla.**) - You don't have to come.

## 5. Negating "To Be" and "To Have"

### A. Negating "To Be" (Existence)
* **Present:** **Illa**
    * ಅವನು ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇಲ್ಲ. (**Avanu maneyalli illa.**) - He is not home.
* **Past:** **Iralilla**
    * ನಾನು ಹುಷಾರಾಗಿ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ. (**Nānu huṣārāgi iralilla.**) - I was not well.
* **Future:** **Iralla**
    * ನಾಳೆ ನಾನು ಇರಲ್ಲ. (**Nāḷe nānu iralla.**) - I won't be there tomorrow.

### B. Negating "To Have" (Possession)
Kannada uses the Dative case + Illa.
* **Rule:** Dative Subject + Object + **Illa**
* **Example:** ನನಗೆ ಸಮಯವಿಲ್ಲ. (**Nanage samayavilla.**) - I don't have time.

---

## 6. The "Without" Form (Negative Gerund)

This form is equivalent to the English "Without doing X".
* **Rule:** Verb Root + **-ade**

| Verb | Kannada | Transliteration | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hēḷu** | ಅವಳು ಹೇಳದೆ ಹೋದಳು. | **Avaḷu hēḷade hōdaḷu.** | She left without telling. |
| **Nillu** | ನಿಲ್ಲದೆ ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದೆ. | **Nillade maḷe bartā ide.** | Rain is coming without stopping. |

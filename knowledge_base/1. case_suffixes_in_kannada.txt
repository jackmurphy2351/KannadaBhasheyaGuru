
# A Grammar Guide to Case Suffixes (ವಿಭಕ್ತಿ ಪ್ರತ್ಯಯಗಳು)

## 1. The "Magic Glue": The Oblique Stem

Before adding a case suffix, you often need to modify the noun slightly. This modified form is called the **Oblique Stem**.

### The "D" Insertion (Neuter 'a' nouns)
If a non-living noun ends in short **-a** (e.g., *Mara* - Tree), you insert a **-d-** before the ending.
* *Mara* + *alli* (in) = **Maradalli** (Not *Maraalli*)
* *Pustaka* + *inda* (from) = **Pustakadinda**

### The "in" Insertion (Nouns ending in 'u')
If a noun ends in **-u** (e.g., *Ūru* - Town), you often change it to **-in-**.
* *Ūru* + *alli* = **Ūrinalli**

## 2. The Seven Cases (Vibhaktis)

Here is the master table. In Kannada, these cover every role a noun can play.

| Case Name | Kannada Name | Function | Suffix (Formal) | Suffix (Colloquial) | Latin Equiv. |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nominative** | Prathama | The Subject | (u) / None | None | Nominative |
| **Accusative** | Dwitiya | The Object | **-annu** | **-nna** / None | Accusative |
| **Instrumental** | Tritiya | By/With (Means) | **-inda** | **-inda** | Ablative (Instr) |
| **Dative** | Chaturthi | To/For/Possession | **-ge** / **-kke** | **-ge** / **-kke** | Dative |
| **Genitive** | Shashthi | Of/'s (Relation) | **-a** | **-a** | Genitive |
| **Locative** | Saptami | In/At (Location) | **-alli** | **-alli** | Locative |
| **Vocative** | Sambhodhane | Calling Out | **-ē** | **-ē** / **-ā** | Vocative |

## 3. Deep Dive: The Critical Cases

### A. Nominative (The Doer)
The noun is in its dictionary form.
* **Example:** ರಾಮ ಬಂದನು. (*Rāma bandanu*) - Rama came.
* **Example:** ಮರ ಬಿತ್ತು. (*Mara bittu*) - The tree fell.

### B. Accusative (The Target) - Suffix: *-annu*
Used when the verb is acting upon the noun.
* **Rule:** In spoken Kannada, you can drop this suffix for inanimate objects. For humans, you **must** use it.
* **Formal:** ನಾನು ಪುಸ್ತಕವನ್ನು ಓದಿದೆ. (*Nānu pustakavannu ōdide.*) - I read the book.
* **Spoken:** ನಾನು ಪುಸ್ತಕ ಓದಿದೆ. (*Nānu pustaka ōdide.*)
* **Human (Mandatory):** ನಾನು ಅವನನ್ನು ನೋಡಿದೆ. (*Nānu avanannu nōḍide.*) - I saw him. (Never "*Nānu avanu nōḍide*").

### C. Instrumental / Ablative (The Means/Source) - Suffix: *-inda*
Kannada groups "By means of" and "From" under the same suffix.
* **Means (Instrumental):** ನಾನು ಕೈಯಿಂದ ಬರೆದೆ. (*Nānu kaiyinda barede.*) - I wrote with (my) hand.
* **Source (Ablative):** ನಾನು ಅಮೇರಿಕದಿಂದ ಬಂದೆ. (*Nānu Amērikadinda bande.*) - I came from America.
* **Cause:** ಮಳೆಯಿಂದ ಟ್ರಾಫಿಕ್ ಇದೆ. (*Maḷeyinda ṭrāphik ide.*) - There is traffic because of rain.

### D. Dative (The Recipient & "Haver") - Suffix: *-ge* / *-ige* / *-kke*
This is the most distinctively "Dravidian" case. It is used for direction, but also for feelings and possession.

* **Direction:** ನಾನು ಮನೆಗೆ ಹೋದೆ. (*Nānu manege hōde.*) - I went to the house.
* **Possession:** ನನಗೆ ಬೆಕ್ಕು ಇದೆ. (*Nanage bekku ide.*) - To me, a cat exists. (I have a cat).
* **Feeling:** ಅವನಿಗೆ ಹಸಿವು ಆಗಿದೆ. (*Avanige hasivu āgide.*) - To him, hunger has happened. (He is hungry).

#### The "-ge" vs. "-kke" Rule:
1.  **Humans/Living:** Always **-ge** or **-ige**. (*Rāmanige*, *Gīthege*).
2.  **Neuter nouns ending in 'u' or 'i':** **-ge**. (*Ūrige* - to town, *Manege* - to house).
3.  **Neuter nouns ending in 'a':** **-kke**. (*Marakke* - to the tree, *Kelasakke* - to work).

### E. Genitive (The Owner) - Suffix: *-a*
Connects two nouns.
* **Example:** ಇದು ರಾಮನ ಮನೆ. (*Idu Rāmana mane.*) - This is Rama's house.
* **Example:** ಮರದ ಎಲೆ. (*Marada ele.*) - Tree's leaf (Leaf of the tree).

### F. Locative (The Container) - Suffix: *-alli*
Simple "in" or "at".
* **Example:** ಅವಳು ಆಫೀಸಿನಲ್ಲಿ ಇದ್ದಾಳೆ. (*Avaḷu āphīsinalli iddāḷe.*) - She is in the office.
* **Example:** ಬೆಂಗಳೂರಲ್ಲಿ ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದೆ. (*Beṅgaḷūralli maḷe bartā ide.*) - Rain is coming in Bangalore.

### G. Vocative (The Call) - Suffix: *-ē* / *-ā*
Used to get attention.
* **Example:** ರಾಮನೇ! ಇಲ್ಲಿ ಬಾ. (*Rāmanē! illi bā.*) - Hey Rama! Come here.
* **Spoken:** ಗುರುಗಳೇ (*Gurugaḷē* - Oh Teacher/Master).

## 4. Plurals and Cases

When a noun is plural, the case suffix attaches **after** the plural marker (*-gaḷu*).

**Formula:** Noun + *gaḷu* + Case Suffix

* **Nominative:** ಮರಗಳು (*Maragaḷu* - Trees)
* **Accusative:** ಮರಗಳನ್ನು (*Maragaḷannu* - The trees)
* **Dative:** ಮರಗಳಿಗೆ (*Maragaḷige* - To the trees) *Note: gaḷu + ge becomes gaḷige*
* **Locative:** ಮರಗಳಲ್ಲಿ (*Maragaḷalli* - In the trees)

## 5. Everyday Examples (Conversational)

| Scenario | Kannada Sentence | Breakdown | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Work** | ನಾನು ಕೆಲಸಕ್ಕೆ ಹೋಗ್ತೀನಿ. | *Kelasa* (Work) + *kke* (to) | I go to work. |
| **Shopping** | ಅಂಗಡಿಯಲ್ಲಿ ಹಣ್ಣು ಇದೆ. | *Aṅgaḍi* (Shop) + *yalli* (in) | There is fruit in the shop. |
| **Traffic** | ಬಸ್ಸಿನಲ್ಲಿ ಜಾಗ ಇಲ್ಲ. | *Bassu* (Bus) + *in* + *alli* (in) | No space in the bus. |
| **Family** | ಅಮ್ಮನ ಫೋನ್ ಬಂತು. | *Amma* (Mom) + *na* (of/Poss) | Mom's phone (call) came. |
| **Data** | ಕಂಪ್ಯೂಟರಿನಿಂದ ಕಳಿಸಿದೆ. | *Kampyūṭar* (Computer) + *inda* (by) | Sent by/from the computer. |


# Kannada Grammar Guide: Relative Clauses & Participles
**(Translating "Who," "That," and "Which")**

## 1. The Core Concept

In English and Hindi, we use relative pronouns (*who, that, which, jo, jis*) to connect a verb to a noun (e.g., "The man **who** came").

In Kannada, **relative pronouns do not exist.** Instead, Kannada turns the entire verb phrase into an **Adjective** and places it **before** the noun.

**The Golden Rule:**
* **English:** Noun + [Relative Pronoun] + Verb
* **Kannada:** [Verb-turned-Adjective] + Noun

---

## 2. Forming the Verbal Adjective (Relative Participle)

To turn a verb into an adjective, attach the appropriate suffix to the verb root or stem.

### A. The Three Main Forms

| Context | Tense | Suffix | Formulation | Example (Root: Māḍu) | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Habit / Future** | Present/Future | **-uva** | Root + uva | **Māḍuva** | The doing / The one who does |
| **Completed** | Past | **-a** | Past Stem + a | **Māḍida** | The done / The one who did |
| **Negative** | Any | **-ada** | Root + ada | **Māḍada** | The not doing / The one who didn't |

### B. Common Irregular Verbs

| Root | Meaning | Present/Future (-uva) | Past (-a) | Negative (-ada) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Baru** | To come | **Baruva** | **Banda** | **Bārada** |
| **Hōgu** | To go | **Hōguva** | **Hōda** | **Hōgada** |
| **Iru** | To be | **Iruva** | **Idda** | **Illada*** |
| **Nōḍu** | To see | **Nōḍuva** | **Nōḍida** | **Nōḍada** |

* *Note: "**Illada**" is the specific negative relative participle for "Iru" (To be).*

---

## 3. Basic Sentence Examples

### Present/Future (The "-uva" Clause)
* **English:** The bus **that goes** to the office.
* **Kannada Logic:** [Office-to going] + bus.
* **Kannada:** ಕಚೇರಿಗೆ ಹೋಗುವ ಬಸ್.
    * **Kacērige hōguva bas.**

### Past (The "-da" Clause)
* **English:** The food **(that) you cooked**.
* **Kannada Logic:** [You cooked] + food.
* **Kannada:** ನೀವು ಮಾಡಿದ ಊಟ.
    * **Nīvu māḍida ūṭa.**

### Negative (The "-ada" Clause)
* **English:** The song **(that) she did not sing**.
* **Kannada Logic:** [She not-singing] + song.
* **Kannada:** ಅವಳು ಹಾಡದ ಹಾಡು.
    * **Avaḷu hāḍada hāḍu.**

---

## 4. The Participial Noun ("The One Who...")

If you remove the noun and replace it with a pronoun suffix, you create a noun meaning "The person who does X" or "The thing that did X."

**Formula:** `Relative Participle` + `Pronoun Suffix`

| Gender | Suffix | Example (Past Tense of Baru) | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Male** | **-avanu** | **Bandavanu** | The man who came / The guest (M) |
| **Female** | **-avaḷu** | **Bandavaḷu** | The woman who came / The guest (F)
| **Plural** | **-avaru** | **Bandavaru** | Those who came / The guests |
| **Neuter** | **-iddu** | **Bandiddu** | That which came / The arrival |

**Usage Example:**
* **English:** "I don't know the person who is going."
* **Kannada:** ಹೋಗುವವನು ನನಗೆ ಗೊತ್ತಿಲ್ಲ.
    * **Hōguvavanu nanage gottilla.**

---

## 5. Advanced: Nested & Complex Clauses

You can stack these clauses to describe complex relationships.

### A. The "Possession" Clause
When describing an object someone has with them:
* **English:** The book **that is with you**.
* **Kannada:** ನಿಮ್ಮ ಹತ್ತಿರ ಇರುವ ಪುಸ್ತಕ.
    * **Nimma hattira iruva pustaka.**

### B. The "Comparison" Clause
When comparing one object to another described object:
* **English:** A man who has a bottle **like the one you have**.
* **Kannada:** [ನಿಮ್ಮ ಹತ್ತಿರ ಇರುವ] ಬಾಟಲ್ [ಥರದ್ದೇ ಬಾಟಲ್ ಇಟ್ಟಿರುವ] ಮನುಷ್ಯ.
    * **[Nimma hattira iruva] bāṭal [tharaddē bāṭal iṭṭiruva] manuṣya.**
    * *Lit: [Your near existing] bottle [like-that-only bottle keeping] man.*

### C. Nominalizing "What I did..."
To talk about an action as a concept (e.g., "My writing," "What I wrote"):
1.  Take the Past Participle (**Bareda**).
2.  Add **Iddu** (The thing/fact).
3.  Add Case Suffix (e.g., Accusative **-annu**).

* **English:** Translating **what I wrote**.
* **Kannada:** ನಾನು ಬರೆದದ್ದನ್ನು ಅನುವಾದಿಸುವುದು.
    * **Nānu baredaddannu anuvādisuvudu.**
    * *(Bareda + iddu + annu = Baredaddannu)*

---

## 6. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. Don't use "Endu/Anta" for Relative Clauses
* *Endu/Anta* means "that" only for quotes or names (e.g., "He said **that**...", "The city called Mysore").
* For descriptions ("The car **that** is red"), you **must** use the *iruva* participle.

### 2. Adjective vs. Noun Form
* **Bareda (Adjective):** Must be followed by a noun.
    * *Bareda pustaka* (Written book).
* **Barediddu/Baredaddu (Noun):** Stands alone.
    * *Nānu barediddu* (What I wrote).
* **Error:** Do not say ~~"Barediddu pustaka."~~

### 3. Possession: Nimage vs. Hattira
* Use **Nimage** for feelings, body parts, or relatives.
    * *Nimage kōpa ide* (You have anger).
* Use **Hattira + Iruva** for portable objects used in relative clauses.
    * *Nimma hattira iruva phōn* (The phone that is with you).

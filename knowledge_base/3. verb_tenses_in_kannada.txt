A Grammar Guide to Kannada Verb Conjugation (ಕಾಲಗಳು ಮತ್ತು ಕ್ರಿಯಾಪದಗಳು)

Overview

In Kannada, mastering the verb is 80% of the battle. Once you know the root and the ending, you can construct almost any sentence.
The Golden Formula:


$$\text{Verb Root} + \text{Tense Marker} + \text{Pronominal Ending} = \text{Conjugated Verb}$$

1. The Pronominal Endings (The "Tail")

These are the suffixes that tell you who is doing the action. They attach to the end of the verb. Because these endings are unique to the person, you can technically drop the pronoun (Subject) and still be understood (just like in Latin Amo vs. Ego amo).
Person	Pronoun	Meaning	Ending (Formal/Written)	Ending (Colloquial/Spoken)
1st Sing	ನಾನು (Nānu)	I	-ಏನೆ (-ēne)	-ಈನಿ (-īni)
2nd Sing	ನೀನು (Nīnu)	You (informal)	-ಇಯ (-iya)	-ಯ / -ಇ (-ya / -i)
2nd Hon	ನೀವು (Nīvu)	You (polite/pl)	-ಇರಿ (-iri)	-ತೀರಿ (-tīri)
3rd Masc	ಅವನು (Avanu)	He	-ಆನೆ (-āne)	-ಆನೆ (-āne)
3rd Fem	ಅವಳು (Avaḷu)	She	-ಆಳೆ (-āḷe)	-ಆಳೆ (-āḷe)
3rd Neut	ಅದು (Adu)	It	-ಅದೆ (-ade)	-ಅದೆ (-ade) / -ಉತ್ತೆ (-utte)
1st Pl	ನಾವು (Nāvu)	We	-ಏವೆ (-ēve)	-ತೀವಿ (-tīvi)
3rd Pl	ಅವರು (Avaru)	They (human)	-ಆರೆ (-āre)	-ತಾರೆ (-tāre)

2. The Present Tense (Non-Past)

Usage: Used for habits, general truths, and future actions.
Note: In spoken Kannada, we rarely use a separate "Future Tense." If you say "I go to the shop," it implies you will go.
The Marker: -ಉತ್ತ (-utta)
Structure:


$$\text{Root} + \text{utta} + \text{Ending}$$
Example: ಮಾಡು (Māḍu - To do)
Pronoun	Written/Formal	Spoken/Conversational	Meaning
ನಾನು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಏನೆ (Māḍuttēne)	ಮಾಡ್ತೀನಿ (Māḍtīni)	I do / I will do
ನೀನು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತೀಯ (Māḍuttīya)	ಮಾಡ್ತಿಯ (Māḍtiya)	You do
ನೀವು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತೀರಿ (Māḍuttīri)	ಮಾಡ್ತೀರಿ (Māḍtīri)	You (resp) do
ಅವನು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಆನೆ (Māḍuttāne)	ಮಾಡ್ತಾನೆ (Māḍtāne)	He does
ಅವಳು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಆಳೆ (Māḍuttāḷe)	ಮಾಡ್ತಾಳೆ (Māḍtāḷe)	She does
ಅದು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಅದೆ (Māḍuttade)	ಮಾಡುತ್ತೆ (Māḍutte)	It does
ನಾವು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಏವೆ (Māḍuttēve)	ಮಾಡ್ತೀವಿ (Māḍtīvi)	We do
ಅವರು	ಮಾಡಉತ್ತಆರೆ (Māḍuttāre)	ಮಾಡ್ತಾರೆ (Māḍtāre)	They do
🚀 Quick Tip for Hindi Speakers:
Think of the Kannada -utta marker as the Hindi -ta (karta, jata).
●	Hindi: Main karta hoon.
●	Kannada: Nānu māḍutta ēne (Maḍtini).

3. The Past Tense

Usage: Completed actions.
The Marker: usually -ದ (-da), but irregulars exist.

Group A: Regular Verbs (The 'u' verbs)
Most verbs ending in 'u' take a -d- sound.
●	ಮಾಡು (māḍu) → ಮಾಡಿದ (māḍida) + ending
Group B: Irregular / Strong Verbs
These must be memorized, but they are the most common verbs.
Verb Root	Meaning	Past Stem	Example (I did...)
ಮಾಡು (māḍu)	To do	ಮಾಡಿದ (māḍida)	ನಾನು ಮಾಡಿದೆ (Nānu māḍide)
ಬರು (baru)	To come	ಬಂದ (banda)	ನಾನು ಬಂದೆ (Nānu bande)
ಹೋಗು (hōgu)	To go	ಹೋದ (hōda)	ನಾನು ಹೋದೆ (Nānu hōde)
ತಿನ್ನು (tinnu)	To eat	ತಿಂದ (tinda)	ನಾನು ತಿಂದೆ (Nānu tinde)
ಇರು (iru)	To be	ಇದ್ದ (idda)	ನಾನು ಇದ್ದೆ (Nānu idde)
ಕಾಣು (kāṇu)	To see	ಕಂಡ (kaṇḍa)	ನಾನು ಕಂಡೆ (Nānu kaṇḍe)
ಕೊಡು (koḍu)	To give	ಕೊಟ್ಟ (koṭṭa)	ನಾನು ಕೊಟ್ಟೆ (Nānu koṭṭe)
Structure:


$$\text{Past Stem} + \text{Ending (Short form)}$$

(Note: In Past tense, the 'I' ending is usually just 'e' not 'ene')
Example: ಹೋಗು (Hōgu - To go) → Stem: ಹೋದ (Hōda)
●	ನಾನು ಹೋದೆ (Nānu hōde) - I went
●	ನೀವು ಹೋದ್ರಿ (Nīvu hōdri) - You went (Plural/Resp)
●	ಅವನು ಹೋದ (Avanu hōda) - He went
●	ಅವಳು ಹೋದಳು (Avaḷu hōdaḷu) - She went
●	ಅದು ಹೋಯಿತು (Adu hōyitu) - It went (Irregular neuter ending)

4. The Present Continuous (Right Now)

Usage: Actions happening at this very moment.
Structure:


$$\text{Root} + \text{tā} + \text{iru (conjugated)}$$

The Formula:
1.	Take Verb Root (e.g., ಬರು - Baru)
2.	Add -tā (Barta - coming)
3.	Add "To Be" (Iddīni / Iddāne / etc.)
Person	Kannada (Spoken)	Meaning
I am...	ನಾನು ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದ್ದೀನಿ (Nānu bartā iddīni)	I am coming.
You are...	ನೀವು ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದ್ದೀರಿ (Nīvu bartā iddīri)	You are coming.
He is...	ಅವನು ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದ್ದಾನೆ (Avanu bartā iddāne)	He is coming.
She is...	ಅವಳು ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದ್ದಾಳೆ (Avaḷu bartā iddāḷe)	She is coming.
It is...	ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದೆ (Maḷe bartā ide)	It is raining (Rain is coming).

5. Important Verbs: To Be (Iru) vs. To Become (Āgu)

These two confuse everyone.
A. ಇರು (Iru) - Existence / Location
Used for "I am here," "I have," "It is in the box."
●	Present: ನಾನು ಇಲ್ಲೇ ಇದ್ದೀನಿ (Nānu illē iddīni) - I am right here.
●	Past: ನಾನು ನೆನ್ನೆ ಊರಲ್ಲಿ ಇದ್ದೆ (Nānu nenne ūralli idde) - I was in town yesterday.
B. ಆಗು (Āgu) - To Happen / To Become
Used for events, feelings, or finishing things.
●	Present/Future: ಅದು ಆಗುತ್ತೆ (Adu āgutte) - It will happen / It happens.
●	Past: ಅದು ಆಯ್ತು (Adu āytu) - It happened / It is finished.
Conversational Gem:
When you finish a task (like eating or working), Kannadigas don't say "I finished." They say "It happened."
●	ಊಟ ಆಯ್ತಾ? (Ūṭa āytā?) - Literal: Did meal happen? (Meaning: Have you eaten?)
●	ಹೂ, ಆಯ್ತು. (Hū, āytu.) - Yes, it happened. (Meaning: Yes, I ate.)

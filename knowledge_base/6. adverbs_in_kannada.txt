# A Grammar Guide to Kannada Adverbs
**(The "-āgi" Rule)**

## 1. The "Magic Suffix": -āgi (-ಆಗಿ)

In English, we often turn an Adjective into an Adverb by adding **-ly** (Quick $\rightarrow$ Quickly).
In Kannada, we do the exact same thing by adding **-āgi** (-ಆಗಿ) to a Noun or Adjective.

* **Origin:** This suffix comes from the past participle of **Āgu** (To become).
* **Literal Logic:** "Having become [Adjective], do [Action]."

### Sandhi Rules (Connecting the Words)
Just like with Adjectives, we insert a "glide letter" to connect the words to avoid a vowel clash.

1.  **The 'V' Glide:** If the word ends in **-a**, add **-v-** (*-vāgi*).
2.  **The 'Y' Glide:** If the word ends in **-i** or **-e**, add **-y-** (*-yāgi*).

### The Core Transformation Table

| Root Word | Meaning | + āgi (Adverb) | Meaning | Usage Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cenna** | Fine / Good | **Cennāgi** | Nicely / Well | **Cennāgi ōdi.**<br>(Study well.) |
| **Sulabha** | Easy | **Sulabhavāgi** | Easily | **Nānu sulabhavāgi gedde.**<br>(I won easily.) |
| **Khuṣi** | Happy | **Khuṣiyāgi** | Happily | **Avaru khuṣiyāgi iddāre.**<br>(They are [living] happily.) |
| **Jōru** | Loud / Fast | **Jōrāgi** | Loudly / Forcefully | **Jōrāgi mātāḍi.**<br>(Speak loudly.) |
| **Nidhāna** | Slow | **Nidhānavāgi** | Slowly | **Nidhānavāgi hōgi.**<br>(Go slowly.) |
| **Spaṣṭa** | Clear | **Spaṣṭavāgi** | Clearly | **Nanage spaṣṭavāgi kēḷisilla.**<br>(I didn't hear clearly.) |

## 2. The "How Are You?" Exception

This is a critical conversational point.

In English, "I am well" is the grammatically correct way to describe your health or state, though "I am good" is extremely common.
Kannada **strictly** enforces the "Well" logic. You cannot use the adjective "Good" (*Cenna*) to describe your state; you **must** use the adverb "Well" (*Cennāgi*).

* **Question:** Hēgiddīra? (How are you?)
* **Wrong Answer:** ~~Nānu cenna iddīni.~~ (I am good person existing.)
* **Correct Answer:** **Nānu cennāgi iddīni.**
    * *Literal Meaning:* I am existing **in a fine way** / I am doing well.
    * *Sandhi Note:* In spoken Kannada, this often contracts to **Nānu cennāgiddīni.**

**Note:** If you are describing an object (like a movie or food), you use the **Adjective + Āgide** rule.
* **Kāphi cennāgide.** (The coffee is good.)
* **Nānu cennāgi iddīni.** (I am well.)

## 3. Essential "Native" Adverbs

Not all adverbs need the *-āgi* suffix. Some words are born as adverbs.

| Adverb | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bēga** | Fast / Early | **Bēga banni.** (Come fast/early.) |
| **Tuṃbā** | Very / Too much | **Tuṃbā dhanyavādagaḷu.** (Many thanks.) |
| **Bahaḷa** | Very (Formal) | **Idu bahaḷa mukhya.** (This is very important.) |
| **Summane** | Simply / Quietly | **Summane kuḷitukoḷḷi.** (Sit quietly / Just sit.) |
| **Mātra** | Only | **Nanage kāphi mātra bēku.** (I want only coffee.) |

---

## 4. Time Adverbs (Temporal Markers)

These are crucial for storytelling (sequencing events).

| Adverb | Meaning | Context |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Īga** | Now | **Īga hōgōṇa.** (Let's go now.) |
| **Āga** | Then | **Āga kareṇṭ iralilla.** (Then, there was no power.) |
| **Āmēle** | Afterwards / Later | **Āmēle phōn māḍuttēne.** (I will phone later.) |
| **Muñce** | Before / Earlier | **Ūṭakke muñce.** (Before the meal.) |
| **Yāvāgalū** | Always | **Avanu yāvāgalū taḍavāgi baruttāne.** (He always comes late.) |

---

## 5. Turning Nouns into Feelings with -āgi

You can use *-āgi* to describe **how** you feel physically or emotionally.

* **Noun:** **Kōpa** (Anger) $\rightarrow$ **Kōpavāgi** (Angrily).
    * *Avaru kōpavāgi nōḍidaru.* (He looked [at me] angrily.)
* **Noun:** **Sustu** (Tiredness) $\rightarrow$ **Sustāgi** (Tiredly/Tired).
    * *Nanage sustāgide.* (To me, it is existing tiredly $\rightarrow$ I feel tired). (*Sustāgi* + *ide* = **Sustāgide**)
* **Noun:** **Huccu** (Madness) $\rightarrow$ **Huccāgi** (Madly).
    * *Avaru huccāgi prītistāre.* (They love madly.)

---

## 6. Summary Checklist

When speaking, ask yourself:
1.  Am I describing a **Thing** (Noun)? $\rightarrow$ Use **Adjective** (e.g., *Doḍḍa mane*).
2.  Am I describing an **Action** (Verb)? $\rightarrow$ Use **-āgi** (e.g., *Jōrāgi mātāḍu*).
3.  Am I saying "**I am well**"? $\rightarrow$ Use **Cennāgi** (not *Cenna*).

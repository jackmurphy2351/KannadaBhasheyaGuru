A Grammar Guide to Conjunctions
(Connecting Your Thoughts)
1. Overview
In Kannada, connectors usually come between two full sentences.
2. The Basic Connectors: And, Or, But
A. And: ಮತ್ತು (Mattu) / ಹಾಗೂ (Hāgū)
Mattu: The standard word for "And." Used to connect nouns or sentences.
Hāgū: Means "As well as" or "Also." It is slightly more formal or emphatic.
Spoken Tip: In fast conversation, people often drop Mattu. instead, they use Jotege (With/Along with) or just pause.
Context
Kannada
English
Nouns
ಕಾಫಿ ಮತ್ತು ಟೀ.

(Coffee mattu tea.)
Coffee and tea.
Sentences
ನಾನು ಬಂದೆ ಮತ್ತು ಅವಳು ಹೋದಳು.

(Nānu bande mattu avaḷu hōdaḷu.)
I came and she left.

B. Or: ಅಥವಾ (Athavā)
Hindi Equivalent: Ya / Athva.
Usage: Used to present choices.
Context
Kannada
English
Choice
ಕಾಫಿ ಅಥವಾ ಟೀ ಬೇಕಾ?

(Coffee athavā tea bēkā?)
Do you want Coffee or Tea?
Plan
ನಾಳೆ ಅಥವಾ ನಾಡಿದ್ದು.

(Nāḷe athavā nāḍiddu.)
Tomorrow or day-after.

C. But: ಆದರೆ (Ādare)
Hindi Equivalent: Lekin / Par.
Note: As mentioned in the Conditional guide, Ādare technically means "If it becomes," but it is the standard word for "But."
Context
Kannada
English
Contrast
ನನಗೆ ಇಷ್ಟ, ಆದರೆ ಸಮಯ ಇಲ್ಲ.

(Nanage ishta, ādare samaya illa.)
I like it, but I don't have time.
Fact
ಇದು ಹಳೆಯದು, ಆದರೆ ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿದೆ.

(Idu haḷeyadu, ādare chennāgide.)
This is old, but it is nice.

3. The Logic Loop: Reason and Result
This is the most important section for daily conversation (explaining why you did something).
A. Because: ಯಾಕೆಂದರೆ (Yākendare)
Spoken Variations: Yākandre / Yāke-andre.
Literal Meaning: "Why... means..." (Why -> Yāke, Means -> Andare).
Structure: [Result Sentence] + [Yākandre] + [Reason Sentence].
Kannada Sentence
Breakdown
English
ನಾನು ಲೇಟ್, ಯಾಕೆಂದರೆ ಟ್ರಾಫಿಕ್ ಇತ್ತು.

(Nānu late, yākandre traffic ittu.)
I am late, why-means traffic was there.
I am late because there was traffic.
ಅವನು ಬರಲ್ಲ, ಯಾಕೆಂದರೆ ಅವನಿಗೆ ಹುಷಾರಿಲ್ಲ.

(Avanu baralla, yākandre avanige hushārilla.)
He won't come, why-means he is unwell.
He won't come because he is unwell.

B. So / Therefore: ಅದಕ್ಕೆ (Adakkē)
Formal Version: Āddarinda (Therefore).
Spoken Version: Adakkē (To that / For that reason).
Structure: [Reason Sentence] + [Adakkē] + [Result Sentence].
Kannada Sentence
Breakdown
English
ಟ್ರಾಫಿಕ್ ಇತ್ತು, ಅದಕ್ಕೆ ನಾನು ಲೇಟ್.

(Traffic ittu, adakkē nānu late.)
Traffic was there, to-that I am late.
There was traffic, so/that's why I am late.
ನನಗೆ ಹಸಿವು, ಅದಕ್ಕೆ ಊಟ ಮಾಡಿದೆ.

(Nanage hasivu, adakkē ūṭa māḍide.)
Hunger to me, that's why I did meal.
I was hungry, so I ate.

4. The "If Not / Otherwise" Connector
This is very common in Bangalore Kannada when making plans.
ಇಲ್ಲಾಂದ್ರೆ (Illāndre)
Literal: Illa (No) + Andre (If said/meant).
Meaning: Otherwise / If not / Or else.
Hindi Equivalent: Nahi toh.
Context
Kannada
English
Warning
ಈಗ ಬನ್ನಿ, ಇಲ್ಲಾಂದ್ರೆ ನಾವು ಹೋಗ್ತೀವಿ.

(Īga banni, illāndre nāvu hōgtīvi.)
Come now, otherwise we will go.
Alternative
ನಾಳೆ ಸಿಗೋಣ, ಇಲ್ಲಾಂದ್ರೆ ಫೋನ್ ಮಾಡಿ.

(Nāḷe sigōṇa, illāndre phone māḍi.)
Let's meet tomorrow, if not, call me.

5. Summary: The Conversation Flow
Here is how you string it all together.
Step 1: Start a thought. -> Nanage bekkugaḷu ishta. (I like cats.)
Step 2 (Contrast): Use Ādare. -> ...ādare nanage bekku illa. (...but I don't have a cat.)
Step 3 (Reason): Use Yākandre. -> ...yākandre nanna apārṭmeṇṭ chikkadu. (...because my apartment is small.)
Step 4 (Result): Use Adakkē. -> ...adakkē nānu sadhyakke tagonḍilla. (...so/that's why I haven't taken one for now.)

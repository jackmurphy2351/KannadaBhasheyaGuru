A Grammar Guide to Conditional Sentences in Kannada
(ಷರತ್ತುಬದ್ಧ ವಾಕ್ಯಗಳು)

Overview

In Kannada, conditional "if... then..." statements are not formed with a separate word for "if." Instead, the "if" clause is created by adding a specific suffix directly to the verb.
The "then" clause is simply the result, and its tense (present, future, or past) determines the meaning of the entire conditional statement.
There are two primary conditional suffixes:
1.	-ರೆ (-re): The Positive Conditional ("If...")
2.	-ದಿದ್ದರೆ (-diddare): The Negative Conditional ("If... not...")

1. The Positive Conditional ("If..."): The -ರೆ Suffix

This is the most common way to say "If [something happens]."
How to Form:
Take the Past Tense Verb Stem and add the suffix -ರೆ (-re).
●	The Past Tense Stem is the form of the verb you use to build the past tense (e.g., ಮಾಡಿದ - māḍida, "did").
Examples of Formation:
Verb Root	Past Tense Stem	Conditional Form (+ರೆ)	Meaning
ಮಾಡು (māḍu) - to do	ಮಾಡಿದ (māḍida)	ಮಾಡಿದರೆ (māḍidare)	If (one) does
ಬರು (baru) - to come	ಬಂದ (banda)	ಬಂದರೆ (bandare)	If (one) comes
ಹೋಗು (hōgu) - to go	ಹೋದ (hōda)	ಹೋದರೆ (hōdare)	If (one) goes
ಕುಡಿ (kuḍi) - to drink	ಕುಡಿದ (kuḍida)	ಕುಡಿದರೆ (kuḍidare)	If (one) drinks
ಇರು (iru) - to be	ಇದ್ದ (idda)	ಇದ್ದರೆ (iddare)	If (one) is
Key Feature: This conditional form (ಮಾಡಿದರೆ, ಬಂದರೆ, etc.) is universal. It does not change for the person (I, you, he, she, they), number, or gender.

2. The Negative Conditional ("If... not..."): The -ದಿದ್ದರೆ Suffix

This form is used to say "If [something does not happen]."
How to Form:
Take the Verb Root and add the suffix -ದಿದ್ದರೆ (-diddare).
Examples of Formation:
Verb Root	Negative Conditional (+ದಿದ್ದರೆ)	Meaning
ಮಾಡು (māḍu)	ಮಾಡದಿದ್ದರೆ (māḍadiddare)	If (one) does not do
ಬರು (baru)	ಬರದಿದ್ದರೆ (baradiddare)	If (one) does not come
ಹೋಗು (hōgu)	ಹೋಗದಿದ್ದರೆ (hōgadiddare)	If (one) does not go
ತಿನ್ನು (tinnu) - to eat	ತಿನ್ನದಿದ್ದರೆ (tinnadiddare)	If (one) does not eat
ಇರು (iru) - to be	ಇರದಿದ್ದರೆ (iradiddare)	If (one) is not

3. Understanding Tense in Conditional Sentences

The "if" clause (with -ರೆ or -ದಿದ್ದರೆ) remains constant. The tense of the main (consequence) clause defines the type of condition.

A. General Truth / Habitual Action (If... then...)

●	Structure: [Conditional Clause] + [Main Clause in Simple Present Tense]
●	Use: To express habits, scientific facts, or general truths.
●	Example:
○	ಬೆಕ್ಕಿಗೆ ಹಸಿವು ಆದರೆ, ಅದು ಮ್ಯಾಕ್ ಮಾಡುತ್ತದೆ.
○	(Bekkige hasivu ādare, adu myāc māḍuttade.)
○	If the cat is hungry, it meows.

B. Future Possibility (If... then...)

●	Structure: [Conditional Clause] + [Main Clause in Future Tense]
●	Use: This is the most common use, discussing future possibilities.
●	Example:
○	ನಾಳೆ ಮಳೆ ಬಂದರೆ, ನಾನು ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇರುತ್ತೇನೆ.
○	(Nāḷe maḷe bandare, nānu maneyalli iruttēne.)
○	If it rains tomorrow, I will stay at home.

C. Command or Suggestion (If... then...)

●	Structure: [Conditional Clause] + [Main Clause in Imperative (Command) or Hortative (Let's...)]
●	Use: Giving advice, instructions, or making suggestions.
●	Example (Command):
○	ನಿನಗೆ ಸುಸ್ತು ಆದರೆ, ವಿಶ್ರಾಂತಿ ತೆಗೆದುಕೊ.
○	(Ninage sustu ādare, viśrānti tegeduko.)
○	If you are tired, take rest.
●	Example (Suggestion):
○	ಹವಾಮಾನ ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿ ಇದ್ದರೆ, ಪಾರ್ಕಿಗೆ ಹೋಗೋಣ.
○	(Havāmāna cennāgi iddare, pārkige hōgōṇa.)
○	If the weather is good, let's go to the park.

D. Past Unreal / Counterfactual ("If... had... then... would have...")

●	Structure: [Conditional Clause] + [Main Clause in Past Conditional Tense (e.g., ...ತ್ತಿದ್ದೆ, ...ತ್ತಿತ್ತು)]
●	Use: To talk about things that did not happen in the past (hypotheticals).
●	Example (Positive):
○	ನೀನು ನನಗೆ ಹೇಳಿದ್ದರೆ, ನಾನು ಸಹಾಯ ಮಾಡುತ್ತಿದ್ದೆ.
○	(Nīnu nanage hēḷidare, nānu sahāya māḍuttidde.)
○	If you had told me, I would have helped.
●	Example (Negative):
○	ನೀನು ಓದದಿದ್ದರೆ, ಪರೀಕ್ಷೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಫೇಲ್ ಆಗುತ್ತಿದ್ದೆ.
○	(Nīnu ōdadiddare, parīkṣeyalli phēl āguttidde.)
○	If you hadn't studied, you would have failed the exam.

E. Present Unreal / Subjunctive ("If... were... then... would...")

●	Structure: (Often same as Past Unreal) [Conditional Clause] + [Main Clause in Past Conditional Tense]
●	Use: To talk about a present situation that is hypothetical or untrue.
●	Example:
○	ನಾನು ನೀನು ಆಗಿದ್ದರೆ, ನಾನು ಅದನ್ನು ಮಾಡುತ್ತಿರಲಿಲ್ಲ.
○	(Nānu nīnu āgiddare, nānu adannu māḍuttiralilla.)
○	If I were you, I would not be doing that.

4. Special Verbs: ಇರು (to be) and ಆಗು (to become)

These two verbs are extremely common in conditional sentences.
Verb	Positive Conditional	Meaning	Negative Conditional	Meaning
ಇರು (iru)	ಇದ್ದರೆ (iddare)	"if (it) is..."	ಇರದಿದ್ದರೆ (iradiddare)	"if (it) is not..."
ಆಗು (āgu)	ಆದರೆ (ādare)	"if (it) becomes..."	ಆಗದಿದ್ದರೆ (āgadiddare)	"if (it) does not become..."
Example (ಇರು):
●	ಡೇಟಾ ಸರಿ ಇದ್ದರೆ, ವಿಶ್ಲೇಷಣೆ ಸರಿಯಾಗಿರುತ್ತದೆ.
●	(Ḍēṭā sari iddare, viślēṣaṇe sariyāgiruttade.)
●	If the data is correct, the analysis will be correct.
Example (ಆಗು):
●	ನನಗೆ ಸಮಯ ಸಿಕ್ಕರೆ, ನಾನು ನಿಮ್ಮನ್ನು ಭೇಟಿ ಮಾಡುತ್ತೇನೆ.
●	(Nanage samaya sikkare...) - Wait, this is ಸಿಗು (sigu).
●	ನನಗೆ ಹಸಿವು ಆದರೆ, ನಾನು ತಿನ್ನುತ್ತೇನೆ.
●	(Nanage hasivu ādare, nānu tinnuttēne.)
●	If I become hungry (lit: if hunger happens to me), I will eat.

5. Important Note: ಆದರೆ (ādare) — A Dual-Meaning Word

This is a critical point that can confuse learners. The word ಆದರೆ (ādare) has two distinct meanings, but context makes them easy to separate.
1.	Conditional ಆದರೆ (ādare): "if it becomes"
○	This is the conditional form of ಆಗು (āgu), as we learned above.
○	Example: ಮಳೆ **ಆದರೆ**, ನಾವು ಬರುವುದಿಲ್ಲ. (Maḷe ādare, nāvu baruvudilla.) - "If it becomes rain (i.e., if it rains), we will not come."
2.	Conjunction ಆದರೆ (ādare): "but"
○	This is used to connect two contrasting sentences.
○	Example: ಮಳೆ ಬರುತ್ತಿದೆ, **ಆದರೆ** ನಾವು ಹೋಗುತ್ತೇವೆ. (Maḷe baruttide, ādare nāvu hōguttēve.) - "It is raining, but we are going."
You will almost never confuse these in a real sentence. The conditional ಆದರೆ is part of the "if" clause, while the conjunction ಆದರೆ separates two complete thoughts.

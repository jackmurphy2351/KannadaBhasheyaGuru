# Impersonal Verbs in Kannada: Infinitives and Verbal Nouns

## Overview
In Kannada, verbs can be modified to function not just as actions, but as nouns or abstract concepts. This module covers two essential forms:
1. **The Infinitive:** "To do," "To eat" (Ending in *-alu*)
2. **The Verbal Noun:** "Doing," "Eating" (Ending in *-uvudu*)

These are called "Impersonal" because they do not change based on who is speaking (I, You, He, She). They remain constant regardless of gender or number.

---

## Part 1: The Infinitive Form (*-alu*)

The infinitive form corresponds to the English "To [verb]" (e.g., to go, to see). It is used to express purpose, permission, or capability.

### Formation Rule
Take the verb root and add the suffix **-alu**.
* If the root ends in *u*, replace the *u* with **-alu**.
* If the root ends in *e* or *i*, add **-yalu**.

### Common Examples

| Verb Root | Meaning | Infinitive (To...) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** | To Do | **Māḍalu** |
| **Bare** | To Write | **Bareyalu** |
| **Ōdu** | To Read | **Ōdalu** |
| **Kuḍi** | To Drink | **Kuḍiyalu** |
| **Tinnu** | To Eat | **Tinnalu** |
| **Nōḍu** | To See | **Nōḍalu** |
| **Kēḷu** | To Ask/Hear | **Kēḷalu** |
| **Hōgu** | To Go | **Hōgalu** |
| **Baru** | To Come | **Baralu** |
| **Iru** | To Be | **Iralu** |
| **Āgu** | To Become | **Āgalu** |

### Usage Examples

**1. Expressing Purpose (Why did you do it?)**
* **English:** I went to see him.
* **Kannada:** Nānu avanannu **nōḍalu** hōde.
    * *(Lit: I him to-see went.)*

**2. Expressing Capability (With 'Āgu')**
* **English:** I cannot do this. (Lit: To do this is not possible for me.)
* **Kannada:** Nanage idu **māḍalu** āgalla.

**3. Expressing Permission (With 'Biḍi' or 'Koḍi')**
* **English:** Let me go. (Lit: Leave me to go.)
* **Kannada:** Nanna **hōgalu** biḍi.

---

## Part 2: The Verbal Noun (*-uvudu*)

The verbal noun turns an action into a concept or an object. It corresponds to the English gerund (verb + ing), such as "Swimming," "Running," or "Reading."

### Formation Rule
Take the verb root and add the suffix **-uvudu**.
* If the root ends in *e* or *i*, add **-yuvudu**.

### Common Examples

| Verb Root | Meaning | Verbal Noun (Doing...) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** | To Do | **Māḍuvudu** |
| **Bare** | To Write | **Bareyuvudu** |
| **Ōdu** | To Read | **Ōduvudu** |
| **Kuḍi** | To Drink | **Kuḍiyuvudu** |
| **Tinnu** | To Eat | **Tinnuvudu** |
| **Nōḍu** | To See | **Nōḍuvudu** |
| **Hōgu** | To Go | **Hōguvudu** |

### Usage Examples

**1. As the Subject of a Sentence**
* **English:** Swimming is good for health.
* **Kannada:** **Ījuvudu** ārōgyakke oḷḷeyadu.
* **English:** Writing in Kannada is difficult.
* **Kannada:** Kannaḍadalli **bareyuvudu** kaṣṭa.

**2. As the Object of a Sentence**
* **English:** I must practice writing.
* **Kannada:** Nānu **bareyuvudannu** abhyāsa māḍabēku.
    * *(Note: 'Bareyuvudu' takes the accusative suffix 'annu' here.)*

**3. With Case Suffixes (Advanced Usage)**
Because these are nouns, you can attach case endings (dative, ablative, etc.) to them.

* **For doing (Dative -kke):**
    * **English:** I came for working (to work).
    * **Kannada:** Nānu kelasa **māḍuvudakke** bande.
* **From doing (Ablative -inda):**
    * **English:** You learn by (from) speaking.
    * **Kannada:** Nīvu **mātāḍuvudarinda** kalitīri.

# A Grammar Guide to Conditional Sentences in Kannada
**(The "If... Then..." Construction)**

## 1. Overview

In Kannada, there is no separate word for "if." Instead, the "if" clause is created by adding a specific suffix directly to the verb.

There are two primary conditional forms:
1.  **Positive Conditional:** "If it happens" $\rightarrow$ Suffix **-are** (added to Past Stem).
2.  **Negative Conditional:** "If it does not happen" $\rightarrow$ Suffix **-adiddare**.

---

## 2. The Positive Conditional ("If...")

**The Golden Rule:**
Take the **Past Tense Stem** of the verb and add **-are** (often written as **-re** if the stem ends in a vowel).

* *Note:* Since most Past Stems end in 'a', adding *-are* simply changes the final 'a' to 'are'.

| Verb Root | Past Stem | + Suffix | Conditional Form | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** (Do) | Māḍida | + are | **Māḍidare** | If (one) does |
| **Baru** (Come) | Banda | + are | **Bandare** | If (one) comes |
| **Hōgu** (Go) | Hōda | + are | **Hōdare** | If (one) goes |
| **Kuḍi** (Drink) | Kuḍida | + are | **Kuḍidare** | If (one) drinks |
| **Iru** (Be) | Idda | + are | **Iddare** | If (one) is / exists |
| **Sigu** (Get) | Sikka | + are | **Sikkare** | If (one) gets/finds |

**Key Feature:** This form (*Māḍidare*, *Bandare*) is **universal**. It does not change for person, gender, or number.

---

## 3. The Negative Conditional ("If... not...")

This form is constructed by taking the **Negative Stem** (Root + a) and adding the conditional of "To Be" (*Iddare*).

**Formula:** `Verb Root` + `a` + `diddare`
*(Literally: "If it is [in the state of] not doing.")*

| Verb Root | Negative Stem | Conditional Form | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Māḍu** | Māḍa | **Māḍadiddare** | If (one) does not do |
| **Baru** | Bara | **Baradiddare** | If (one) does not come |
| **Hōgu** | Hōga | **Hōgadiddare** | If (one) does not go |
| **Tinnu** | Tinna | **Tinnadiddare** | If (one) does not eat |
| **Iru** | Ira | **Iradiddare** | If (one) is not |

---

## 4. Understanding Tense in "Then" Clauses

The "if" clause (*-are*) remains constant. The meaning changes based on the **Main Verb** in the second half of the sentence.

### A. General Truth (If X, then Y happens)
* **Structure:** Conditional + Simple Present/Future
* **Example:** ಬೆಕ್ಕಿಗೆ ಹಸಿವು ಆದರೆ, ಅದು ಮ್ಯಾವ್ ಮಾಡುತ್ತದೆ.
    * **Bekkige hasivu ādare, adu myāv māḍuttade.**
    * (If the cat becomes hungry, it meows.)

### B. Future Possibility (If X, then I will Y)
* **Structure:** Conditional + Future Tense
* **Example:** ನಾಳೆ ಮಳೆ ಬಂದರೆ, ನಾನು ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇರುತ್ತೇನೆ.
    * **Nāḷe maḷe bandare, nānu maneyalli iruttēne.**
    * (If it rains tomorrow, I will stay at home.)

### C. Command/Suggestion (If X, then do Y)
* **Structure:** Conditional + Imperative
* **Example:** ನಿನಗೆ ಸುಸ್ತು ಆದರೆ, ವಿಶ್ರಾಂತಿ ತೆಗೆದುಕೊ.
    * **Ninage sustu ādare, viśrānti tegeduko.**
    * (If you get tired, take rest.)
* **Example:** ಹವಾಮಾನ ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿ ಇದ್ದರೆ, ಪಾರ್ಕಿಗೆ ಹೋಗೋಣ.
    * **Havāmāna cennāgi iddare, pārkige hōgōṇa.**
    * (If the weather is good, let's go to the park.)

### D. Past Unreal / Regret (If I had X, I would have Y)
To express things that *didn't* happen, use the **Past Continuous** form (*-uttidde*) in the main clause.

* **Positive Example:**
    * ನೀನು ನನಗೆ ಹೇಳಿದ್ದರೆ, ನಾನು ಸಹಾಯ ಮಾಡುತ್ತಿದ್ದೆ.
    * **Nīnu nanage hēḷiddare, nānu sahāya māḍuttidde.**
    * (If you **had told** me, I **would have helped**.)
* **Negative Example:**
    * ನೀನು ಓದದಿದ್ದರೆ, ಪರೀಕ್ಷೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಫೇಲ್ ಆಗುತ್ತಿದ್ದೆ.
    * **Nīnu ōdadiddare, parīkṣeyalli phēl āguttidde.**
    * (If you **hadn't studied**, you **would have failed**.)

---

## 5. Important Note: The Two "Ādare"s

Learners often get confused because **Ādare** appears in two very different contexts.

### 1. The Conditional "If"
* **Origin:** *Āgu* (to become) + *are* = *Ādare*.
* **Meaning:** If it happens / If it becomes.
* **Usage:** ಮಳೆ **ಆದರೆ**, ನಾವು ಹೋಗುವುದಿಲ್ಲ.
    * *Maḷe **ādare**, nāvu hōguvudilla.* (If rain happens, we won't go.)

### 2. The Conjunction "But"
* **Origin:** Same etymology, but frozen as a connector.
* **Meaning:** However / But.
* **Usage:** ಮಳೆ ಬರುತ್ತಿದೆ, **ಆದರೆ** ನಾವು ಹೋಗುತ್ತೇವೆ.
    * *Maḷe baruttide, **ādare** nāvu hōguttēve.* (It is raining, **but** we are going.)

**How to tell them apart?**
* If *Ādare* is attached to the first clause ("If X..."), it is a Conditional.
* If *Ādare* separates two contradictory facts ("X is true, BUT..."), it is a Conjunction.

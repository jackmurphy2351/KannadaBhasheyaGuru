Comprehensive Guide to Kannada Negation (ಕನ್ನಡದಲ್ಲಿ ನಿಷೇಧ)


1. The Core Distinction: Alla (ಅಲ್ಲ) vs. Illa (ಇಲ್ಲ)

This is the most important rule in Kannada negation.

A. ಅಲ್ಲ (Alla) - Negation of Identity / Quality

Use ಅಲ್ಲ (alla) to negate nouns or adjectives. It means "is not this/that." It's used when you are correcting a fact about what something is.
●	Rule: (Subject) + (Noun/Adjective) + ಅಲ್ಲ (alla)
●	Example 1: ಇದು ನನ್ನ ಮನೆ ಅಲ್ಲ. (Idu nanna mane alla.)
○	"This is not my house." (It is some other house).
●	Example 2: ನಾನು ಎಪಿಡೆಮಿಯಾಲೊಜಿಸ್ಟ ಅಲ್ಲ. (Nānu epidemiologist alla.)
○	"I am not an epidemiologist." (I am some other profession).
●	Example 3: ಆಹಾರ ಖಾರ ಅಲ್ಲ. (Āhāra khāra alla.)
○	"The food is not spicy." (It is some other flavor, like mild or sweet).

B. ಇಲ್ಲ (Illa) - Negation of Existence / Action

Use ಇಲ್ಲ (illa) to negate verbs or existence. It means "is not present," "does not exist," or "does not do."
●	Rule: (Subject) + (Location/Verb) + ಇಲ್ಲ (illa)
●	Example 1: ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಯಾರೂ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Maneyalli yārū illa.)
○	"There is no one in the house." (Existence).
●	Example 2: ನನಗೆ ಸಮಯ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Nanage samaya illa.)
○	"I don't have time." (Literally: "To me, time does not exist").
●	Example 3: ಅವನು ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡಲ್ಲ. (Avanu kelasa māḍalla.)
○	"He does not work." (Action - see Section 2).

2. Negating Verbs (Simple Tenses)

The best part of simple negative tenses is that the verb form does not change for any pronoun (I, you, he, she, we, they).

A. Simple Present & Future Tense ("don't do" / "won't do")

In conversation, the present and future negatives are the same. Context (like ನಾಳೆ - tomorrow) clarifies the tense.
●	Rule (Formal): Verb Root + ಉವುದಿಲ್ಲ (-uvudilla)
●	Rule (Colloquial): Verb Root + ಅಲ್ಲ (-alla)
●	Example 1 (māḍu - to do): ನಾನು ಕಾಫಿ ಮಾಡಲ್ಲ. (Nānu coffee māḍalla.)
○	"I don't make / won't make coffee."
●	Example 2 (baru - to come): ಅವರು ನಾಳೆ ಬರಲ್ಲ. (Avaru nāḷe baralla.)
○	"They will not come tomorrow."

B. Simple Past Tense ("did not do")

This form is also universal for all pronouns.
●	Rule: Verb Root + ಲಿಲ್ಲ (-lilla) (Note: The li is added to a stem, e.g., māḍu -> māḍa-lilla, baru -> bara-lilla, tinnu -> tinna-lilla)
●	Example 1 (kēḷu - to ask): ಅವಳು ನನ್ನನ್ನು ಕೇಳಲಿಲ್ಲ. (Avaḷu nannannu kēḷalilla.)
○	"She did not ask me."
●	Example 2 (kalahisu - to send): ನಾವು ಈಮೇಲ್ ಕಳುಹಿಸಲಿಲ್ಲ. (Nāvu email kaḷuhisalilla.)
○	"We did not send the email."

3. Negating Verbs (Aspects: Continuous & Perfect)


A. Present Continuous Tense ("is not doing")

Used for actions not happening right now.
●	Rule: Verb Root + ಉತ್ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ (-uttā illa) or (colloquial) + ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ (-tā illa)
●	Example 1 (maḷe baru - to rain): ಈಗ ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Īga maḷe bartā illa.)
○	"It is not raining right now."
●	Example 2 (malagu - to sleep): ಬೆಕ್ಕು ಮಲಗ್ತಾ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Bekku malagtā illa.)
○	"The cat is not sleeping."

B. Past Continuous Tense ("was not doing")

Used for actions that were not in progress at a specific time in the past.
●	Rule: Verb Root + ಉತ್ತಾ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ (-uttā iralilla) or (colloquial) + ತಾ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ (-tā iralilla)
●	Example 1 (kelasa māḍu - to work): ನಿನ್ನೆ ಈ ಸಮಯದಲ್ಲಿ ನಾನು ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡ್ತಾ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ. (Ninne ī samayadalli nānu kelasa māḍtā iralilla.)
○	"I was not working at this time yesterday."

C. Present Perfect Tense ("have not done")

This is a very common and important form. It's different from the Simple Past. ಮಾಡಲಿಲ್ಲ (māḍalilla) means "I didn't do it" (at a specific time). ಮಾಡಿಲ್ಲ (māḍilla) means "I haven't done it" (yet, or as a state).
●	Rule: Past Participle Stem (ends in i or e) + ಇಲ್ಲ (illa)
●	Example 1 (māḍu - to do): ನಾನು ಇನ್ನೂ ನನ್ನ ಕೆಲಸ ಮಾಡಿಲ್ಲ. (Nānu innū nanna kelasa māḍilla.)
○	"I have not done my work yet."
●	Example 2 (hōgu - to go): ಅವಳು ಬೆಂಗಳೂರಿಗೆ ಹೋಗಿಲ್ಲ. (Avaḷu Beṅgaḷūrige hōgilla.)
○	"She has not been (gone) to Bangalore."

D. Past Perfect Tense ("had not done")

Used to show something had not happened before another past event.
●	Rule: Past Participle Stem + ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ (iralilla)
●	Example 1 (baru - to come): ನಾನು ಮನೆಗೆ ಬರುವ ಮುಂಚೆ, ಅವರು ಬಂದಿರಲಿಲ್ಲ. (Nānu manege baruva mun̄ce, avaru bandiralilla.)
○	"Before I came home, they had not come."

4. Negating Moods & Common Constructions


A. Imperative ("Don't...")

Used for commands.
●	Rule (Informal/Singular): Verb Root + ಬೇಡ (bēḍa)
●	Rule (Formal/Plural): Verb Root + ಬೇಡಿ (bēḍi)
●	Example 1: ಅಲ್ಲಿಗೆ ಹೋಗಬೇಡ! (Allige hōgabēḍa!) - "Don't go there!"
●	Example 2: ಚಿಂತೆ ಮಾಡಬೇಡಿ. (Cinte māḍabēḍi.) - "Don't worry." (polite)

B. Prohibitive ("Should not...")

Used for general rules or moral prohibitions.
●	Rule: Verb Root + ಬಾರದು (bāradu)
●	Example 1: ಸುಳ್ಳು ಹೇಳಬಾರದು. (Suḷḷu hēḷabāradu.)
○	"One should not tell lies."
●	Example 2: ಆ ರೀತಿ ಮಾತಾಡಬಾರದು. (Ā rīti mātāḍabāradu.)
○	"One should not speak like that."

C. Potential / Ability ("Cannot...")

Used to express inability.
●	Rule: Infinitive (Root + ಅಲು or ಅಕ್ಕೆ) + ಆಗಲ್ಲ (āgalla) / ಆಗುವುದಿಲ್ಲ (āguvudilla)
●	Example 1 (arthamāḍiko - to understand): ನನಗೆ ಇದು ಅರ್ಥಮಾಡಿಕೊಳ್ಳಲು ಆಗಲ್ಲ. (Nanage idu arthamāḍikoḷḷalu āgalla.)
○	"I cannot understand this."
●	Example 2 (baru - to come): ಅವನಿಗೆ ಪಾರ್ಟಿಗೆ ಬರಕ್ಕೆ ಆಗಲ್ಲ. (Avanige pārṭige barakke āgalla.)
○	"He cannot come to the party."

D. Desire ("Don't want...")

●	Rule: Dative Subject (e.g., ನನಗೆ) + Noun/Verb + ಬೇಡ (bēḍa)
●	Example 1 (Noun): ನನಗೆ ಕಾಫಿ ಬೇಡ. (Nanage coffee bēḍa.)
○	"I don't want coffee."
●	Example 2 (Verb): ನನಗೆ ಹೋಗಲು ಬೇಡ. (Nanage hōgalu bēḍa.)
○	"I don't want to go." (Less common than "hōgabēḍa" - don't go)

E. Obligation ("Don't have to..." / "Not necessary")

●	Rule: ...ಬೇಕಾಗಿಲ್ಲ (bēkāgilla) or ಬೇಕಿಲ್ಲ (bēkilla)
●	Example 1: ನೀವು ಬರಬೇಕಾಗಿಲ್ಲ. (Nīvu barabēkāgilla.)
○	"You don't have to come."

5. Negating "To Be" and "To Have"


A. Negating "To Be" (State / Existence)

●	Present: ಇಲ್ಲ (illa)
○	ಅವನು ಮನೆಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Avanu maneyalli illa.) - "He is not at home."
●	Past: ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ (iralilla)
○	ನೆನ್ನೆ ನಾನು ಚೆನ್ನಾಗಿ ಇರಲಿಲ್ಲ. (Nenne nānu cennāgi iralilla.) - "I was not well yesterday."
●	Future: ಇರಲ್ಲ (iralla) / ಇರುವುದಿಲ್ಲ (iruvudilla)
○	ನಾಳೆ ನಾನು ಕಚೇರಿಯಲ್ಲಿ ಇರಲ್ಲ. (Nāḷe nānu kaceriyalli iralla.) - "I will not be in the office tomorrow."

B. Negating "To Have" (Possession)

Kannada uses the dative case for possession. The negation is simply ಇಲ್ಲ (illa).
●	Rule: Dative Subject + Noun + ಇಲ್ಲ (illa)
●	Example 1: ನನಗೆ ಸಮಯ ಇಲ್ಲ. (Nanage samaya illa.) - "I don't have time."
●	Example 2: ಅವರಿಗೆ ಮಕ್ಕಳು ಇಲ್ಲ. (Avarige makkaḷu illa.) - "They don't have children."

6. Other Important Negative Forms


A. "Without" (Negative Gerund)

This form is equivalent to "without doing..."
●	Rule: Verb Root + ಅದೆ (-ade)
●	Example 1: ಅವಳು ಹೇಳದೆ ಹೋದಳು. (Avaḷu hēḷade hōdaḷu.)
○	"She left without telling."
●	Example 2: ನಿಲ್ಲದೆ ಮಳೆ ಬರ್ತಾ ಇದೆ. (Nillade maḷe bartā ide.)
○	"Rain is coming without stopping."
